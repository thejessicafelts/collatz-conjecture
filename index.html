Collatz Conjecture

The Collatz conjecture is a conjecture in mathematics that is named after Lothar Collatz, who first introduced the idea in 1937. It concerns sequences of numbers, defined as follows:

  1. Start with any positive integer, n.

  2. Then each term is obtained from the previous term as follows:

    1. If the previous term is even, the next term is one half of the previous term: n = (n / 2).

    2. If the previous term is odd, the next term is 3 times the previous term, plus 1: n = ((n * 3) + 1).

  3. The conjecture state that no matter what the starting value of n is, the sequence will always reach 1.
